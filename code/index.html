I'm Joyce Kuo.

I'm a guitarist, web developer, and a passionate advocate for spreading awareness about the harpejji, a truly extraordinary and versatile electric stringed instrument.

I'm not an official representative of the company who manufactures this product. I'm just a musician who had the opportunity to share it with a legend. Here's how it all happened, and how the harpejji could inspire you too.

A New Instrument with Untapped Potential

It was winter 2012, and I was just a local musician who was fortunate enough to attend the National Association of Music Merchants (NAMM) Show, an invitation-only trade event where musicians and industry insiders gather to experience the latest in musical innovation.

While navigating the bustling aisles, an instrument called the harpejji caught my eye. As a guitarist with a background in piano, I was immediately drawn to its unique blend of both instruments. Naturally, I had to give it a try. The harpejji's unique combination of highly sensory interaction and rich sound left a profound impression on me, and I realized it had the potential to make a significant impact on the future of music.

An Unexpected Encounter with Stevie Wonder

Just a few hours later, I found myself chatting with someone connected to Stevie Wonder's inner circle. Before long, I was within earshot of the legend himself and surrounded by security detail, fans, and paparazzi.

As Stevie played a hammer dulcimer, I heard him express a desire to feel more connected to the strings. In that moment, I felt compelled to ask if he had ever played the harpejji. Stevie hadn't heard of it, but he was curious and insisted I take him to this new instrument so he could try it out for himself.

Stevie's Connection to the Harpejji

Watching Stevie Wonder play the harpejji for the first time was a surreal experience. As soon as his fingers touched the strings, it was clear that the instrument spoke to him in a way that few others had. It offered the direct, tactile connection he had been seeking, and his excitement was contagious.

Stevie was eager to have a custom harpejji crafted for himself, and the instrument soon became a cornerstone of his songwriting, recording, and live performances. He even dazzled the audience at the Grammy Awards with an epic harpejji performance, solidifying the instrument's place in his musical legacy.

The Power of Spreading Good Information

The story of the harpejji and Stevie Wonder reminds me that by spreading the right kind of information, we can spark meaningful change and positivity in unexpected ways.

Stevie showcasing its capabilities also inspired a wave of other artists to explore the harpejji's unique sound. Musicians like Cory Henry, Harry Connick Jr, Jordan Rudess of Dream Theater, and film composor A.R. Rahman, to name a few, have each brought their own distinct style to the instrument. From jazz and classical to progressive rock and cinematic scores, the harpejji has proven to be incredibly versatile, adapting to and enhancing a wide array of musical genres.

An Instrument Worthy of More Attention

One challenge with the harpejji is that it is made to order and not something you can easily find in stores, so it remains somewhat under the radar. That's why I've made it my mission to help more people discover this remarkable instrument.

It's not just about shining a spotlight on the harpejji; it's about ensuring that everyone - especially those who might not have access to traditional instruments - has the opportunity to connect with their music in fresh and innovative ways.

Making a Tiny Ripple in the Ocean of Music

Looking back on that day at The NAMM Show and everything that followed, I'm proud to have played a small role in the story of the harpejji, and I'm excited to see where it goes next. The instrument has already changed music for one legend, and I believe it can do the same for many others.
