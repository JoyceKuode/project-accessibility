A Harpejji Story:

The Discovery that Transformed Stevie Wonder's Music

Hi!

I'm Joyce Kuo.

I'm a guitarist, web developer, and a passionate advocate for spreading awareness about the harpejji, an extraordinary and versatile electric stringed instrument.

I'm not a representative of Marcodi, the company who manufactures the instrument. I'm just a musician who had the incredible opportunity to share an instrument with a legend. Here's how it all happened, and how the harpejji could inspire you too.

A New Instrument with Untapped Potential

It was winter 2012, and I was a local musician fortunate enough to attend the National Association of Music Merchants (NAMM), an invitation-only trade show where musicians and industry insiders gather to experience the latest in musical innovation.

As I wandered through the bustling aisles, I stumbled across an instrument I had never seen before: the harpejji. At first glance, it looked like a cross between a piano and a guitar. Its design immediately intrigued me, so I decided to give it a try. I was drawn to the instrument's unique blend of tactile interaction and beautiful sound. I had a feeling that this was more than just another instrument, but it had real potential to break new ground in music

An Unexpected Encounter with Stevie Wonder

Later that day, I found myself chatting with someone connected to Stevie Wonder's inner circle. Before long, I was within earshot of the legend himself as he played a hammer dulcimer, expressing his desire to feel more connected to the strings. It was in that moment that I felt compelled to speak up and I asked if he had ever tried the harpejji. He hadn't heard of it, but Stevie was intrigued and insisted I take him to this new instrument so he could try it out for himself.

Stevie's Connection to the Harpejji

Watching Stevie Wonder play the harpejji for the first time was a surreal experience. As soon as his fingers touched the strings, it was clear that the instrument spoke to him in a way that few others had. It offered the direct, tactile connection he had been seeking, and his excitement was infectious.

Soon after that moment, Stevie had a custom harpejji made for him, and the instrument became a part of his performances and recordings.
